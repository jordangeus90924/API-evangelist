I am fortunate to be able to take part in numerous of very interesting conversations around API strategy, with companies who are trying to understand, and maximize their role in the API economy. However, one side effect of my world, is that most of these conversations begin and end with strategy planning, and when it comes to deployment and management, I rarely continue on with discussions. I do have clients who do check in with me, at different stops along their journey, but for the most part my role ends once the strategy discussions end.

I would like to change this with a new relationship I've established with APIWare, a new API-first agency that is dedicated to defining, deploying, and managing APIs throughout their lifecycle. I have worked with the core team that is APIWare, on earlier projects like the Cashtie API, and after a couple of years, I've learned to trust the work they do.

API Evangelist and APIWare are partnering to make sure that some is always available to help you meet the demand of your API operations, by exclusiving focusing on:

  • Defining Your API - Where do your resources originate, and what is the best path forward in defining and designing your APIs.
  • Developing Your API - The actual hard work of crafting your APIs from scratch, all the way to using common API gateway solutions and frameworks.
  • Managing API Operations - Take care of the day challenges when operating your API from monitoring and availability to developer on-boarding. 

The APIWare team is determined to stay focused in these areas, while you are busy doing other things, and the team is working hard to follow many of my best practices showcase across my work in the API space. Now you don't just get a handful of initial conversations with me about best practices, you have a team to help you address the pieces of API operations that are falling through the cracks.

I will not be doing development, we have a dedicated team who is more skilled than I am, however I will be involved with overall best practices for the team, and will be touching each client's project as it comes in the door, and at regular stages throughout its evolution. This approach helps make sure I can continue conversations throughout an APIs lifecycle, be there to share thoughts on the roadmap, and tell the important stories about API operations from the trenches. 

The API Evangelist + APIWare partnership reflects a shift in how I do things for the next five years. I am looking to capture more of the exhaust from my world--filling a gap in providing the companies I work with a reliable source of API development, and management resources, which operates in a structured, yet agile, project management workflow, that follows an API-first philosophy.

This new evolution in API Evangelist will not change my tone. My goal is to not to build a startup, but to help meet a growing demand in the space. I will still be as opinionated, and honest about my approach, I will just have more stories to tell! ;-)
